About the Project

"Your Portrait, Your Price" is a project created by me, Omii Chen, a Visual Artist who is interested in exploring what artistic value means to people in contemporary society. Through this work, I seek to understand how we assign worth to art beyond traditional market metrics and institutional validation.


This project combines artistic creation with social experimentation. I will draw portraits for participants at art markets, but without setting a fixed price. Participants are free to decide how much they want to pay, or even receive the artwork for free.


The project was partly inspired by the rapid development of AI-generated visual art. In an era where artificial intelligence can quickly produce portraits in any style, the question arises: what gives art its true value? As AI continues to evolve, the worth of art increasingly depends on how we perceive and recognize artistic value beyond mere technical reproduction. This face-to-face portrait project offers a counterpoint to digital creation by emphasizing the human connection, presence, and shared experience that remain uniquely human aspects of art-making.


Through this approach, each transaction becomes a conversation about the value of art. Some may believe art should be shared freely, some might pay according to their financial ability, while others might decide the price based on the emotional resonance the work brings them.


This project is not just about drawing portraits, but a collective exploration of artistic values in our rapidly changing technological landscape. I invite you to participate in this experiment and reflect together on the meaning and value of art in modern society.

The Importance of Live Experience

Why Face-to-Face Portraits Matter

Creating portraits in person is at the heart of this project. This direct interaction creates a unique dimension that cannot be replicated through digital means or photography. Here's why this face-to-face experience is essential:


For the Artist: Drawing someone in person presents both a challenge and an inspiration. It requires careful observation of not just physical features, but also personality and spirit. These observations become integrated into the artwork, capturing something deeper than mere appearance.


For the Art Consumer: Witnessing the creative process unfold provides a powerful sense of presence and connection. You're not just purchasing a product; you're participating in an artistic moment. This immediacy creates a relationship with both the artist and the artwork that goes beyond commercial transaction.


For the Art Experience: The face-to-face portrait session reminds us that no artistic creation is merely a commodity. It's a moment of human connection, translated through artistic skill into a tangible form. This project invites both artists and art consumers to reconsider the commercialization of art and reflect on what gives art its true value.


By participating in this project, you're not simply acquiring a portraitâ€”you're engaging in a dialogue about art's value that begins with human connection and continues long after the drawing is complete.

Alternative Value Exchange

The value of art extends far beyond monetary measurement. In the "Your Portrait, Your Price" project, we embrace a more holistic view of value by welcoming various forms of exchange:

  • Barter & Objects - Perhaps a handcrafted item you created, a book that holds special meaning to you, a local specialty from your hometown, or any object that represents value to you
  • Skill Exchange - Offer your professional skills or knowledge, such as photography services, professional consultation, cooking lessons, musical performances, etc.

Each exchange is a unique story and a reconsideration of the concept of "value." Through diverse exchange methods, we collectively explore how art can transcend commercial frameworks and return to the purest connection between people.


When participating in live events, please tell me how you would like to exchange for your portrait, and let's create an art experience that goes beyond currency.
